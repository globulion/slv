Spherical multipole moment is defined as:
%
\begin{equation}
 q_{l,m} = \int d^3 x  Y^*_{l,m}(\theta, \phi) \rho(\bf r)
\end{equation}
%
where $Y_{l,m}(\theta, \phi)$ is the spherical harmonic function and
$ \rho(\bf r)$ is the charge density distribution.
For example, dipole moments in spherical and Cartesian coordinates are
%
\begin{subequations}
 \begin{align}
 \mu_z &= \sqrt{\frac{4\pi}{3}} q_{1,0} \\
 \mu_x - i \mu_y &= -\sqrt{\frac{8\pi}{3}} q_{1,1}  \\
 \mu_x + i \mu_y &= -\sqrt{\frac{8\pi}{3}} q_{1,-1}  
 \end{align}
\end{subequations}
%
The above equations specify fully the transformation between $\mu_\alpha$ and $q_{1,m}$. Analogous
but more complicated relations exist for quadrupoles and higher moments.
